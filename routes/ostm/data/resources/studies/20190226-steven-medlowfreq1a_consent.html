PARTICIPATION INFORMATION

 

TITLE: Word frequency and contextual diversity effects in short-term memory

PURPOSE OF THE RESEARCH:

This study is being conducted by Steven Roodenrys, Leonie Miller and Dominic Guitard from the University of Wollongong, Australia. The purpose of the research is to investigate the processes underlying verbal short-term memory.  The experiment you will complete is looking at how our ability to recall a list of words is influenced by the number of times a word is used in general language and the number of different contexts in which a word is used.

METHOD & DEMANDS ON PARTICIPANTS:

If you choose to be included in the study, you will be presented with lists of six words on the computer screen, one at a time.  At the end of the list you have to type in the words, in the order in which they were presented; i.e., the first word first, then the second word, then the third word, etc.  You will be presented with 32 lists in this manner (plus two practice lists) and the whole task should take approximately 20 minutes.

 

All your data collected today will be anonymous to ensure your privacy. No identifiable data will be reported on, and only group descriptive and inferential statistics generated from the collected data will be reported. All your data will be stored safely on password protected computer accounts on the Prolific system and the University of Wollongong.

 

POSSIBLE RISKS, INCONVENIENCES & DISCOMFORTS:

Your involvement in this task is voluntary. You are free to refuse to participate or to withdraw your consent to participate. This decision will not affect your relationship with the School of Psychology nor with the University of Wollongong. Though you may decide to withdraw from the study at any time, please be aware that, since the data collected is anonymous, removal of your data after you have completed the serial recall task will not be possible.

 

BENEFITS OF THE RESEARCH:

This research will provide useful information about how short-term memory works and improve our understanding of this fundamental cognitive capacity.  Findings from the study may be published in peer-reviewed journals and/or in a conference presentation/poster.  Your confidentiality is assured and you will not be identified in any part of this research or its publication. You will receive payment for 20 minutes participation through the Prolific system.
